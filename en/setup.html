DeviceAway® Setup

Follow these quick steps to start receiving email notifications.

Step 1:  Attach the Lightning or USB-C Cable to the wall holder with the supplied Nylon tie.  

Step 2:  Mount the phone holder to a wall outside of the bedroom.  If mounting in the Bedroom, look out for USB extension wires that could be used to defeat the purpose of DeviceAway®.  Use either the supplied screws for rough surfaces or the double stick pad If the wall is smooth.   Clean the wall with the alcohol pad.

NOTE:  THE DOUBLE STICK PAD CAN DAMAGE DRYWALL WHEN REMOVED.  USE DENTAL FLOSS TO REMOVE THE PHONE HOLDER EASILY.

Step 3:  Plug the DeviceAway® side labeled "Wall" into a USB wall adapter and plug a phone, wireless charger or laptop to the “Device” side.  

Step 4:  Press the Setup button on back side of DeviceAway®. The red light will turn on, indicating that the Wi-Fi portal is active.   TIP:  If the button does not work, try charging a phone.   Some power adapters need to wake up when a phone or device is connected.  

Step 5:  On your phone, navigate to the Wi-Fi settings and click on the DeviceAway_Setup network. This process is the same for both Android and iPhone devices.

Step 6: Click on Setup to proceed. Choose your wifi network and enter your password. Also, enter the email where DeviceAway® will send the emails to. The recipient email address is never shared and only stored locally.   

Check "Use Wireless Charger" box if DeviceAway® is connected to a wireless charger.

Step 7: Click on Save & Exit when finished.   Sometimes, it needs to be clicked twice.  DeviceAway® will send a SETUP COMPLETE email to the Recipient Address confirming everything is working. 

Troubleshooting:   If the LED light is blinking, it means DeviceAway® can’t send emails.   Count the blinks to see why.

1 Blink – DeviceAway® isn’t connected to wifi.   Run setup again, choose the network and re-enter the password. 
2 Blinks – The phone and wall power adapter are connected backwards.   The phone should connect to the Device side. 
3 Blinks – There is no valid email recipient address.   Run setup again and be sure to click Save. 
4 Blinks – A DeviceAway® has reached the max daily limit of 10 alerts. 
5 Blinks – The recipient email address isn’t formatted correctly.   Run setup again. 
6 Blinks – Wifi is working, but the factory server isn’t accessible.   This is rare.  Check firewalls or if the internet is actually working. 
7 Blinks – Wifi is working, but GitHub is blocked.   Check firewall settings on the router or parental controls. 
8 Blinks – Wifi is working, but the Google is blocked.  Check firewall settings on the router or parental controls on the network.
