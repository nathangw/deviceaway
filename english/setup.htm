ChargeMailer Instructions

 

 

Step 1: Plug the ChargeMailer side labeled "Wall" into a USB Type-C wall adapter.

 

 

 

Step 2: Press the Setup button on ChargeMailer. The LED light will turn on, indicating that the Wi-Fi portal is active.

 

 

 

 

Step 3: On your phone, navigate to the Wi-Fi settings and select the ChargeMailer_Setup network. This process is the same for both Android and iPhone devices.

 

 

 

 

Step 4: Once your phone connects to the ChargeMailer_Setup network, click on it again to access the setup portal. If a popup appears prompting you to sign in, click on it to proceed.

 

 

 

Step 5: Click on Configure WiFi to proceed with the setup.

 

Step 6: Select your Wi-Fi network from the list and enter the Wi-Fi password.

 

 

Step 7: If needed, select a different language. The language will update after Wi-Fi is connected and the system downloads an automatic internet update.

 

Step 7a: Enter a unique name for the charger, such as your childs name. Avoid using apostrophes due to HTML limitations.

 

Step 7b: If you're using a wireless charging station, check Use Wireless Charger. This is important for detecting when the phone is placed on the charger.

 

Step 7c: Enter one or two recipient email addresses where the charging notifications will be sent.

 

Step 7d: The Special Commands section is for advanced users and specific configurations.

 

Step 7e: Click Save and Exit. The red light should turn off. If everything is set up correctly, the red light will remain off, without blinking any error codes.

 

 

 

 

 

Step 8: Connect the phone to a USB-C cable or place it on the wireless charger (if applicable). ChargeMailer is now ready to monitor the phone’s charging and send email notifications.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Error Codes and Troubleshooting:

If ChargeMailer encounters an issue with sending emails or connecting to Wi-Fi, the LED will blink an error code. You can retry the setup at any time by pressing the Setup button and following the previous steps again.

 

1 Blink – Wi-Fi Not Connected

  • Possible Causes:
    • Ensure your Wi-Fi network is working properly.
    • Double-check and re-enter the correct Wi-Fi password during setup.
    • Make sure the device is within full signal range, just like any other Wi-Fi device.

 

4 Blinks – No Phone Charged in 24 Hours

  • Note: This is not an error but an indicator that ChargeMailer hasn’t detected any phone charging activity in the last 24 hours.

 

5 Blinks – Email Address Rejected by Server

  • Possible Causes:
    • Verify that the recipient email address is valid and functional.
    • Ensure there is no punctuation in the email address other than the @ symbol.
    • Redo the setup and carefully enter the email address again.

 

6 Blinks – Email Credentials Not Found on GitHub Server

  • Action: Contact the manufacturer for assistance, as this indicates an issue with retrieving email credentials from the GitHub server.
